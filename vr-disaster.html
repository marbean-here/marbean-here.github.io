VR Disaster Preparedness Simulation

An immersive Unity-based VR application designed to teach students how to respond during disasters.

Unity C# VR 3D Simulation

Project Overview

The VR Disaster Preparedness Simulation is a Unity-based virtual reality application designed to teach proper disaster response through interactive scenarios. The system places users in realistic 3D environments where they can safely experience and practice emergency procedures.

Disaster scenarios vary by context: fire and earthquake simulations take place within a school campus, while the typhoon scenario is set in a residential house prone to flooding. Users are guided through each situation using visual cues, subtitles, and step-by-step instructions to ensure clarity and effective learning.

Key Features

  • Interactive VR campus navigation
  • Fire, earthquake, flood, and typhoon simulations
  • Guided instructions with subtitles and visual cues
  • Wayfinding system with directional arrows
  • Realistic 3D environment

My Role

I independently designed and developed the entire VR disaster preparedness simulation. My responsibilities included 3D modeling of the campus environment, programming game logics and interactions in C#, implementing VR mechanics and wayfinding systems, designing UI and instructional elements, and optimizing performance for a smooth VR experience.

Challenges & Solutions

  • Optimizing performance for VR – solved by reducing polygon counts, using baked lighting, and minimizing real-time effects.
  • Guiding users without overwhelming them – implemented visual arrows, subtitles, and step-by-step prompts.
  • Maintaining realism while ensuring user comfort – balanced visual effects with safety-focused simulations.

What I Learned

  • VR interaction and user experience design principles
  • Unity performance optimization for VR environments
  • 3D modeling workflow and scene organization
  • Designing educational simulations using game engines
